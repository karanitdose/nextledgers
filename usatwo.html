Compilation Engagements in the USA

Helping American businesses structure their financials for clarity, compliance, and confidence

What is a Compilation Engagement?

In the United States, a compilation engagement is a service performed by a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) where financial data provided by a client is compiled into structured financial statements. This service is governed by the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA) under the Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services (SSARS).

Unlike audits or reviews, a compilation does not provide assurance on the accuracy or completeness of the financial data. Instead, it offers businesses a formal and professional presentation of their financial position, which can be critical for internal planning, tax reporting, or discussions with stakeholders like banks, landlords, or potential investors.

Purpose of Compilation Engagements

Compilation services serve as a valuable financial reporting tool for small to medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in the U.S. that require well-organized financial statements but are not mandated to undergo an audit or review. The core objectives include:

  • Preparing financial reports for annual income tax filing with the IRS
  • Assisting business owners in monitoring growth and cash flow trends
  • Facilitating SBA loan applications or financing discussions
  • Meeting internal record-keeping and partner reporting needs
  • Complying with less stringent financial disclosure requirements

Key Components

  1. Financial Statement Preparation: Transforming raw financial data into structured financial statements such as the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement.
  2. Client Responsibility: All financial information is provided by the business owner or management, who are solely responsible for its accuracy.
  3. No Audit Procedures: The CPA does not verify the information, make inquiries, or perform analytical procedures.

Who Should Consider a Compilation Engagement?

Many U.S. businesses benefit from compilations as a cost-effective reporting option. These include:

  • Small Businesses: Especially LLCs and sole proprietorships that want professional statements for bank accounts or leasing agreements.
  • Startups: Early-stage companies that need formal financials for fundraising or pitching to angel investors.
  • Real Estate Investors: Needing statements to secure mortgage financing or manage multi-property portfolios.
  • Medical Practices & Professional Services: Seeking reliable reporting for internal planning and third-party partnerships.

Benefits of Compilation Engagements

  • Affordable Financial Reporting: Compilations offer an economical way to maintain structured financials compared to audits.
  • Bank-Ready Reports: Professionally formatted statements that meet lender requirements without additional assurance costs.
  • Quick Turnaround Time: With no fieldwork or verification involved, CPAs can deliver results in a shorter time frame.
  • Tax & Legal Readiness: Statements prepared in accordance with GAAP or tax basis can simplify filings and IRS compliance.

Regulatory Framework

In the United States, compilation engagements are regulated under SSARS No. 21 and follow a streamlined reporting format. CPAs must include a compilation report that outlines their role and clarifies that no assurance is being provided. Businesses may choose the accounting basis (e.g., accrual, cash, or tax basis), depending on their specific needs or industry practices.
