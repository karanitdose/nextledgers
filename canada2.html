Investment Accounting in Canada

Manage and report your investment assets with transparency and compliance

What is Investment Accounting?

Investment accounting involves tracking and managing financial assets like stocks, bonds, and mutual funds in accordance with Canadian financial regulations. It ensures accurate valuation, income recognition, and regulatory reporting of investment portfolios.

Importance for Canadian Entities

This service is essential for corporations, wealth managers, and nonprofit organizations that handle complex portfolios and need reliable, compliant reporting for stakeholders and the CRA (Canada Revenue Agency).

Core Elements

  1. Accurate tracking of investment transactions
  2. Valuation of assets and calculation of gains/losses
  3. Compliance with IFRS or ASPE frameworks

Who Benefits from Investment Accounting?

Investment accounting services are ideal for:

  • Corporations with long-term financial assets
  • Portfolio managers and financial advisors
  • Trusts and foundations managing endowments
  • Private investors requiring detailed reporting

Why It Matters

Proper investment accounting provides decision-makers with reliable data, supports tax efficiency, and enhances financial transparency for audits or external reviews.
